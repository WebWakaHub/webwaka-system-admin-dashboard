## 1. Introduction

This framework defines the principles and processes for identifying, developing, and managing "Suites" on the WebWaka platform. Suites are integrated sets of capabilities designed to solve specific industry vertical problems, providing a comprehensive solution for our partners and tenants.

## 2. Suite Definition

A Suite must meet the following criteria:

- **Vertical Focus:** Addresses the needs of a specific industry (e.g., Commerce, Transportation, Healthcare).
- **Integrated Capabilities:** Combines multiple platform features and services into a cohesive user experience.
- **Partner-Centric:** Enables partners to build and offer services to their own customers (tenants).
- **Monetizable:** Has a clear path to revenue generation through subscriptions, transaction fees, or other models.

## 3. Suite Lifecycle

The lifecycle of a Suite follows four distinct stages:

| Stage | Description | Key Activities |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Identification** | Research and validate potential new suite opportunities. | Market analysis, partner interviews, competitive landscape review. |
| **2. Incubation** | Develop the Minimum Viable Product (MVP) for the suite. | Define core features, build initial architecture, onboard pilot partners. |
| **3. Growth** | Scale the suite by adding features and expanding the user base. | Add advanced capabilities, expand marketing efforts, build a partner ecosystem. |
| **4. Maturity** | Optimize the suite for profitability and market leadership. | Refine pricing, improve operational efficiency, explore new revenue streams. |

## 4. Governance

All new suites must be approved by the Product & Platform Strategy department. A formal proposal document is required, outlining the market opportunity, target audience, core features, and financial projections. This ensures that all platform development aligns with our strategic goals and the 10-Year Platform Roadmap.
