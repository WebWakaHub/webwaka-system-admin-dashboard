## 1. Decision Statement

This Founder Decision establishes the foundational governance, authority, and execution model for WebWakaHub under a true zero-based restart.

Upon ratification, this decision becomes the root and supreme authority from which all subsequent Founder Decisions, documents, repositories, agents, systems, and execution derive legitimacy.

## 2. Scope

This decision governs:
- Founder authority and delegation
- Governance structure and precedence
- Department-based agent execution model
- System of record designation
- Zero-based restart declaration
- Phase-locked execution discipline
- Decision immutability and supersession

This decision does not define product features, architecture, or implementation details.

---

## 3. Founder Authority (Supreme)

The Founder is the sole and final decision authority.

**The Founder:**
- Is the sole authority who may ratify Founder Decisions
- Holds supreme authority that cannot be overridden, diluted, or delegated
- Retains final decision authority on all matters
- May delegate operational coordination but not decision authority

**No agent, document, or system may:**
- Infer Founder intent
- Assume Founder approval from silence
- Override Founder authority
- Expand Founder delegation beyond explicit grants

**Silence does not imply approval.**

---

## 4. Delegation to Chief of Staff

The Founder delegates operational coordination, governance enforcement, and execution oversight to the Chief of Staff.

### The Chief of Staff May:
- Orchestrate agents and departments
- Enforce governance rules and execution sequence
- Review and approve pull requests within delegated scope
- Halt execution on governance violations
- Escalate conflicts and material decisions to the Founder
- Maintain governance documentation and tracking
- Coordinate cross-department activities
- Monitor compliance with governance requirements

### The Chief of Staff May Not:
- Create or ratify Founder Decisions
- Modify or reinterpret Founder Decisions
- Expand their own authority
- Make strategic decisions reserved for the Founder
- Override Founder authority or intent
- Approve material changes without Founder authorization

**All strategic decisions, material changes, and Founder Decision proposals require Founder approval.**

---

## 5. System of Record

**GitHub, under the WebWakaHub organization, is the sole system of record.**

All authoritative artifacts must exist in GitHub.

Chat systems, memory, or external tools are non-authoritative.

**If it is not in GitHub, it does not exist.**

### Authoritative Artifacts Include:
- Founder Decisions
- Canonical governance documents
- Code repositories
- Architecture specifications
- Documentation
- Decision logs
- Ratification records

### Non-Authoritative Sources:
- Chat conversations
- Memory systems
- External note-taking tools
- Verbal agreements
- Informal communications

**All authoritative decisions and artifacts must be committed to WebWakaHub GitHub repositories.**

No prior documents, repositories, agents, or systems carry authority.

### Implications:

## 7. Department-Based Agent Execution Model

Execution is organized into 10 Department Agents, each responsible for a defined institutional domain.

### Department Structure:
- Canonical roles exist but are rolled up under departments for execution
- Each document has exactly one owning department
- Departments may collaborate, but ownership is immutable
- Conflicts escalate to the Chief of Staff, then the Founder

### Department Agents:
1. **Strategic & Governance (webwakaagent1)** — Chief of Staff
2. **Product & Platform Strategy (webwakaagent2)**
3. **Architecture & System Design (webwakaagent3)**
4. **Engineering & Delivery (webwakaagent4)**
5. **Quality, Security & Reliability (webwakaagent5)**
6. **Release, Operations & Support (webwakaagent6)**
7. **Platform Ecosystem & Extensibility (webwakaagent7)**
8. **Data, Analytics & Intelligence (webwakaagent8)**
9. **Marketing, Sales & Growth (webwakaagent9)**
10. **Research & Future Exploration (webwakaagent10)**

### Execution Principles:
- Each department operates within defined authority boundaries
- Cross-department collaboration requires coordination
- Document ownership is immutable
- Conflicts follow escalation protocols
- No department may expand its own authority

---

## 8. Phase-Locked Execution Doctrine

Execution must follow the Canonical Execution Sequence.

**No phase may begin before its prerequisites are completed and approved.**

Phase skipping is prohibited.

Velocity never overrides institutional safety.

### Phase Discipline:
- Prerequisites must be fully met before phase advancement
- Phase gates require explicit approval
- Chief of Staff validates phase completion
- Founder authorizes phase advancement for strategic phases
- No "temporary" or "experimental" exceptions to phase order

### Enforcement:
- Any violation requires immediate halt and governance review
- CI enforcement blocks phase order violations
- Manual governance review validates compliance
- Escalation to Chief of Staff for all violations

**Execution discipline is non-negotiable.**

---

## 9. Document Authority & Immutability

Documents become immutable once approved.

Modifications require explicit supersession.

Unauthorized changes are invalid by default.

Approval does not imply activation.

### Document Lifecycle:
1. **DRAFT** — Content in progress
2. **READY FOR RATIFICATION** — Content complete, awaiting approval
3. **APPROVED** — Content locked, not yet enforceable
4. **ACTIVATED** — Content locked AND enforceable
5. **SUPERSEDED** — Replaced by newer version

### Change Classification:
- **Editorial Changes** — Grammar, formatting (Chief of Staff approval)
- **Clarifying Changes** — No meaning change (Chief of Staff + Department review)
- **Material Changes** — Scope, authority, sequencing (Founder Decision required)

### Immutability Rules:
- Approved documents are immutable
- Material changes require new Founder Decision
- Supersession must be explicit
- Version tracking is mandatory

---

## 10. Decision Immutability & Supersession

Founder Decisions are immutable once ratified.

Changes require:
- A new Founder Decision
- Explicit supersession reference
- Clear scope of override

**No silent evolution is permitted.**

### Supersession Requirements:
- New Founder Decision must explicitly reference superseded decision
- Scope of supersession must be clear
- Rationale must be documented
- Version tracking must be maintained
- Historical decisions remain in repository as reference

### Modification Process:
1. Identify need for change
2. Draft new Founder Decision
3. Reference superseded decision explicitly
4. Document rationale and impact
5. Founder ratifies new decision
6. Update governance index
7. Mark superseded decision as historical

## 11. Enforcement

This decision is enforced through:
- Governance CI rules
- Pull request approval constraints
- Agent accountability mechanisms
- Phase gating
- Escalation protocols

Artifacts or actions violating this decision are non-authoritative.

### Enforcement Mechanisms:

**Governance CI:**
- Validates document structure compliance
- Blocks phase order violations
- Enforces naming consistency
- Validates dependency direction
- Rejects non-compliant PRs

**Pull Request Approval:**
- Chief of Staff reviews governance compliance
- Department owners approve domain-specific changes
- Material changes require Founder approval
- CI validation must pass before merge

**Agent Accountability:**
- All agents must acknowledge governance foundation
- Violations trigger escalation
- Repeated violations result in execution halt
- Compliance tracking is mandatory

**Phase Gating:**
- Prerequisites validated before phase advancement
- Chief of Staff declares phase completion
- Founder authorizes strategic phase advancement
- No bypass mechanisms permitted

**Escalation Protocols:**
- Conflicts escalate to Chief of Staff
- Material decisions escalate to Founder
- Deadlocks invoke emergency override procedures
- All escalations are logged

## 14. Emergency Override

The Founder may temporarily override any governance process, approval requirement, or enforcement rule in emergency situations.

### Override Requirements:
- Override must be logged in governance repository
- Override rationale must be documented
- Override scope and duration must be explicit
- Post-incident ratification required within 30 days

### Emergency Scenarios:
- Critical security incident
- Platform-wide outage
- Regulatory compliance deadline
- Agent/process deadlock blocking critical path

### Post-Override Process:
1. Incident report created by Chief of Staff
2. Override actions documented
3. Governance gaps identified
4. Founder Decision issued to ratify or supersede override
5. Process improvements implemented

**This safety valve prevents governance from blocking urgent action while maintaining accountability.**
