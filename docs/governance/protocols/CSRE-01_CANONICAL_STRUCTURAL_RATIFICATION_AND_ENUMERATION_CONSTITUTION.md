## 2. Definitions

- **Canonical Structure:** A discrete, named, and versioned component of the platform's biological or runtime architecture (e.g., an Organelle, a Cell, a Runtime Service).
- **Canonical Issue:** A single, trackable unit of work directly corresponding to one of the 29 standardized implementation facets of a Canonical Structure.
- **Structural Invariant:** A non-negotiable mathematical or logical rule governing the quantity, naming, or relationship of Canonical Structures and Issues.
- **Dual Structural Invariant:** The core doctrine stating the platform is composed of two distinct but interoperable structural layers: the Biological Layer and the Runtime Layer.
- **Cognitive Appendix:** A set of non-canonical, AI-advisory overlay issues used for analysis, simulation, and meta-governance. These issues are explicitly excluded from all canonical structural mathematics.
