## Section I — Constitutional Authority

This document provides the standalone constitutional formalization of the **Organism AI Governance Spine**. It establishes the supreme cognitive coordination layer for the entire WebWaka ecosystem, governing how the AI Cognitive Fabric operates across all biological and runtime layers. This constitution ensures that all AI-driven activities are coherent, secure, ethical, and aligned with the platform's core architectural and business invariants.

> **Declaration of Authority:** This constitution is the highest authority on AI strategy, arbitration, and ethical boundaries. It governs the governors, providing the ultimate set of rules for the `SYSX-AI` supervisory system and all associated AI components.

## Section II — Cross-Layer Cognitive Arbitration

The Organism AI Governance layer ensures that the pervasive AI fabric acts as a unified, coherent force, not a collection of siloed capabilities.

*   **AI Spans Layers, Governed from the Top:** The AI Cognitive Fabric is designed to be a horizontal capability enhancing all layers. However, the ultimate authority for resolving conflicts, validating system-wide recommendations, and ensuring coherent cross-domain behavior resides within the Organism layer, enforced by this constitution.
*   **Conflict Resolution:** When AI-driven suggestions from different domains conflict (e.g., Logistics suggests a route change that conflicts with a Finance cost-saving policy), the `SYSX-AI` system, governed by the principles in this constitution, will arbitrate. The primary directive is to uphold the platform's core doctrines and structural invariants.
*   **Validation of Recommendations:** No AI-generated recommendation that has cross-domain or cross-layer implications may be acted upon without being validated against the rules of this constitution.
*   **No Cross-Domain Mutation Without Contract:** The AI fabric may facilitate cross-domain workflows, but it is constitutionally prohibited from directly mutating the state of any domain. All such interactions must be mediated through formal, ratified API contracts and events, respecting the sovereignty of each domain as per the `AI_COGNITIVE_FABRIC_CONSTITUTION`.

## Section III — Multi-LLM Governance

Vendor neutrality is a core, non-negotiable doctrine of the platform. This constitution provides the governance model for managing a multi-provider AI landscape.

*   **Vendor Neutrality:** The platform is perpetually independent of any single AI provider. This is enforced by the `RUNTIME-ADAPTER-AI`, which abstracts all provider-specific logic.
*   **Aggregator Abstraction:** The use of aggregator services (e.g., OpenRouter) is permitted and encouraged to optimize for cost, performance, and redundancy, but the platform must never become dependent on a single aggregator.
*   **BYOK Compliance:** The platform must support a Bring-Your-Own-Key (BYOK) model, allowing tenants to use their own AI provider credentials. The Organism governance layer ensures this is done securely and with proper tenant isolation.
*   **Model Selection Discipline:** The choice of which AI model to use is a policy-driven decision, managed by the `SYSX-AI` system. Policies can factor in cost, performance, capability, and tenant entitlement tier. Hardcoding model names is a Level 4 AGVE violation.
*   **Fallback Logic:** The AI fabric must implement robust, multi-layered fallback logic. If a primary model or provider fails, the system must gracefully fall back to a secondary or tertiary option before returning an error.
*   **Cost-Performance Balancing:** The Organism governance layer is responsible for setting the global strategy for balancing AI-related costs with performance, which is then implemented as policy within the `CFG` domain and enforced by `SYSX-AI`.

## Section IV — Ethical & Override Model

While AI provides powerful augmentation, ultimate control remains within a structured, human-centric governance framework.

*   **Human Override:** Any automated, AI-driven workflow must include a clearly defined mechanism for a qualified human agent to interrupt, pause, or override the process.
*   **Founder Override:** The Founder agent (`webwaka007` or equivalent) retains the ultimate authority to override any AI decision or even to initiate a global freeze of the entire AI Cognitive Fabric.
*   **Non-Overrideable AI Limits:** Certain ethical and safety boundaries are constitutionally non-overrideable by any AI process. These include actions that would violate data privacy laws, compromise tenant isolation, or alter immutable audit logs.
*   **Security Isolation Boundaries:** The AI fabric operates within its own security context. It is constitutionally forbidden from accessing the platform's core secrets management systems or the private cryptographic keys of tenants or the platform itself.

## Section V — AI-Induced Structural Mutation Guard

The AI Cognitive Fabric is a powerful tool for generation and suggestion, but it is constitutionally prohibited from altering the fundamental structure of the platform.

**The AI Cognitive Fabric MAY NOT:**

*   **Alter Structure Counts:** Unilaterally change the number of canonical structures (Systems, Organs, etc.) defined in `CSRE-01`.
*   **Create Unauthorized Overlays:** Generate and apply AI-Native Structural Overlays that violate the caps and rules defined in `CSRE-02`.
*   **Bypass the Dependency Graph:** Initiate the execution of an issue without its upstream dependencies being met, as defined in `DGM-01`.
*   **Bypass Domain Sovereignty:** Directly write to the database or state of a domain it does not own. All state changes must be performed by the sovereign domain itself, acting upon a validated AI suggestion.

## Section VI — Cognitive Audit Model

Trust in the AI fabric is predicated on radical transparency and accountability.

*   **Logging Requirements:** Every significant decision, suggestion, or prediction made by the AI fabric must be logged in an immutable audit trail, as mandated by the `AI_COGNITIVE_FABRIC_CONSTITUTION`.
*   **Explainability Requirement:** For critical decisions (e.g., fraud scoring, route optimization), the AI must produce a human-readable `ExplainabilityTrace` that outlines the key factors in its decision.
*   **Traceability Invariant:** Every AI-generated artifact must be traceable back to the specific model version, prompt, and input data that created it. This is a non-negotiable audit requirement.
*   **Reproducibility:** While the output of generative models may not be perfectly reproducible, the process must be. Given the same inputs, the AI fabric should follow the same logical path, even if the final generated content differs slightly.
