## 1. Constitutional Authority

This document is ratified by Founder Authority to establish a new, dual invariant governing the canonical issue count for all structures within the WebWaka platform. It serves as the supreme source of truth for issue enumeration, superseding any conflicting statements in prior constitutional documents or audits regarding per-structure issue counts.

This protocol was necessitated by the findings of the `CSRE-DELTA-01` audit and the subsequent live issue census, which confirmed that the historical "29 Issues Per Structure" invariant was no longer universally accurate due to the integration of the AI Cognitive Fabric.

## 2. Historical Context: The 29-Issue Invariant

The platform was founded on a structural invariant of **29 Canonical Issues Per Structure**. This standard ensured a uniform, predictable, and complete lifecycle for the implementation of every canonical component. This model was historically ratified and enforced by the `CSRE-01` series of constitutions.

## 3. AI Expansion Context

The activation of the **AI Cognitive Fabric** and the implementation of the **DAEF-01 (Deep AI Native Encoding Formula)** introduced a valid, additive expansion of issue counts for specific structures. This expansion, termed the **AI-Native Structural Overlay**, added new capabilities without replacing the foundational 29 issues, thus breaking the historical single invariant.

## 4. Census Findings (142 / 4,772)

A live issue census conducted on 2026-02-24 under the authority of this protocol confirmed the ground truth of the platform:

- **Total Canonical Structures:** 142 (in accordance with CSRE-01 v2.0.0)
- **Total Canonical Issues:** 4,772

The census revealed a bifurcated reality:

- **67 structures** adhere to the historical 29-issue baseline.
- **75 structures** have been expanded with an AI-Native Structural Overlay.

## 5. The Base Core Invariant

Every Canonical Structure, without exception, must contain the **Base Structural Core**.

> **The Base Structural Core consists of exactly 29 Base Structural Issues.**

This invariant is non-negotiable and represents the fundamental, universal implementation lifecycle for any component. No structure may have fewer than 29 issues.

## 6. The Overlay Invariant

To account for AI-native capabilities, this constitution ratifies the **AI-Native Structural Overlay**.

> **The Overlay Invariant is an additive-only layer of issues that may be applied to a Canonical Structure on top of the 29-issue Base Core.**

Overlay rules are as follows:
- **Additive Only:** Overlay issues may only be added; they cannot replace or alter the Base Core.
- **Layer-Justified:** The application and size of an overlay must be justified by the specific functional requirements of a given layer, as defined in this constitution.
- **Non-Arbitrary:** Overlays are not arbitrary and cannot be inflated without a formal constitutional amendment.

## 8. Mathematical Reconciliation Statement

The total issue count of 4,772 is constitutionally reconciled as follows:

- **67 Base Structures:** 67 * 29 = 1,943 issues
- **75 Expanded Structures:**
  - 56 Organs * (29+10) = 2,184 issues
  - 18 Systems * (29+5) = 612 issues
  - 1 Organism * (29+5) = 34 issues
- **Total Issues:** 1,943 + 2,184 + 612 + 34 = **4,772 issues**

This calculation validates that all 4,772 issues are accounted for under the new dual-invariant model.

## 9. Enforcement Clause

All platform governance and automation, particularly the **EIM-01 (Execution Integrity Monitoring Protocol)**, must be updated to enforce this dual-invariant model. Any action that would cause a structure to fall below 29 issues or exceed its layer-authorized overlay cap is prohibited.

## 10. Future Mutation Discipline

Any change to the Base Core count (29) or the Layer-Authorized Overlay Distribution requires a formal constitutional amendment. The process must include, at minimum, a new census audit and Founder ratification.

## 11. Hard Stop Clause

This constitution represents the complete and final word on the canonical issue invariant. No further mutation is permitted without a formal amendment. All agents and protocols must adhere to the dual-invariant model defined herein.
