## SECTION I — CONSTITUTIONAL AUTHORITY

This document establishes the single, canonical source of truth for interpreting legacy domain aliases within the WebWaka Governance Architecture. Its purpose is to reconcile naming drift from previous architectural phases, prevent semantic confusion, and preserve the structural integrity of the canonical domain model.

This is a **governance-definition-only** document. It does not authorize the renaming of issues, the mutation of biological structures, or the merging of domains.

---

## SECTION II — PURPOSE

This registry serves to:

1.  Define the immutable, canonical domain names of the WebWaka architecture.
2.  Define the permitted interpretation of legacy aliases found in historical documents or code.
3.  Explicitly declare that a semantic alias does **not** imply a structural merge.
4.  Prevent semantic corruption and accidental domain collapse.
5.  Enable the accurate interpretation of legacy artifacts without requiring their mutation.

---

## SECTION III — CANONICAL DOMAIN DECLARATION

The following thirteen (13) domains are declared as the complete and sovereign set of canonical domains within the WebWaka architecture. These names are constitutionally protected and may not be altered without a formal Founder-level amendment.

-   **COM** — Commerce
-   **FIN** — Finance
-   **LOG** — Logistics
-   **TRN** — Transportation
-   **RES** — Resource & Asset
-   **GEO** — Geographic & Location
-   **EXP** — Experience & Interaction
-   **ANA** — Analytics
-   **CFG** — Configuration & Policy
-   **IDA** — Identity & Access
-   **SEC** — Security
-   **INF** — Infrastructure
-   **AI** — Artificial Intelligence (Cognitive Fabric)

These canonical domains are, and shall forever remain, sovereign and structurally distinct.

## SECTION V — AI–ANA EXPLICIT SEPARATION DECLARATION

To prevent the most critical form of semantic drift, the distinction between the AI Cognitive Fabric and the Analytics (ANA) domain is permanently and constitutionally sealed.

| Construct | Nature | Core Function |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **AI (Cognitive Fabric)** | Generative & Prospective | Creates, predicts, and reasons about new information. | 
| **ANA (Analytics)** | Aggregative & Retrospective | Measures, projects, and reports on existing information. |

**AI ≠ ANA.** These domains are not interchangeable. Any document, issue, or artifact that conflates them is subject to immediate AGVE review.

---

## SECTION VI — INFRASTRUCTURE VS. INSTRUMENTATION CLARIFICATION

- **`INF` (Infrastructure):** The canonical domain responsible for the Runtime Plane, deployment, observability adapters, logging infrastructure, database bindings, and message brokers.
- **`IN` (Instrumentation):** A legacy alias that is now constitutionally interpreted as the **Infrastructure Observability Subdomain** within `INF`. It is not a sovereign domain.

---

## SECTION VII — EXP VS. UI CLARIFICATION

- **`EXP` (Experience):** The canonical domain responsible for all user-facing interaction and presentation logic.
- **`UI` (User Interface):** A legacy alias that is now constitutionally interpreted as a **presentation aspect** of the `EXP` domain. It is not a sovereign domain.

The `EXP` domain includes, but is not limited to, UI, UX, Website Builders, and Funnel Builders.

---

## SECTION VIII — ALIAS INTERPRETATION RULES

1.  **Semantic Aids Only:** Aliases are tools for interpreting legacy artifacts, not for defining new structures.
2.  **No Dependency Graph Mutation:** An alias does not and cannot alter the canonical dependency graph.
3.  **No Renaming Authority:** This registry does not authorize the renaming of any existing issues, files, or components.
4.  **No Structural Merge:** An alias never implies a structural merge between two canonical domains.
5.  **Canonical Prevails:** In any conflict of interpretation, the canonical domain definition always prevails.
6.  **AGVE Scrutiny:** The AGVE may flag the misuse of an alias as an attempted structural mutation.

---

## SECTION IX — ENFORCEMENT MODEL

- **Level 3 Review:** The AGVE may trigger a Level 3 (Warning) review if it detects the conflation of `AI` and `ANA`, the treatment of `UI` as a canonical domain, or the use of an alias to bypass structural governance.
- **Level 4 Freeze:** The AGVE will trigger a Level 4 (Platform Freeze) if the misuse of an alias results in an actual structural mutation.
