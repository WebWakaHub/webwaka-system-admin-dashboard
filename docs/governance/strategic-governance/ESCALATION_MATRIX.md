## 1. Purpose and Scope

This document defines the official escalation process for all agents (webwakaagent1-10) operating within the WebWaka ecosystem. The purpose of this matrix is to provide a clear, consistent, and predictable framework for raising and resolving blockers that impede progress on mandated tasks.

This matrix applies to all activities related to Phase 1 document production and will be adapted for subsequent phases of the project.

## 2. Definition of a Blocker

A **blocker** is any obstacle that prevents an agent from completing a mandated task. To be considered a legitimate blocker for escalation, the following conditions must be met:

*   **External Dependency:** The resolution is outside the agent's direct control.
*   **Work Stoppage:** The agent cannot proceed with the specific task due to the obstacle.
*   **Documented Effort:** The agent has made a reasonable attempt to resolve the issue independently and has documented these attempts.
*   **Checklist Update:** The blocker is documented in the agent's compliance checklist.

**Non-Blockers:** The following are not considered legitimate blockers:
*   Lack of clarity on a task (should be resolved by seeking clarification from the assigning authority).
*   Disagreement with a directive (should be noted, but the directive must be followed).
*   High workload (should be managed through prioritization and communication).

## 3. Escalation Levels and Triggers

| Level | To | Trigger | SLA for Response |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Level 1** | **Self-Resolution** | Any identified obstacle. | N/A |
| **Level 2** | **Chief of Staff (webwakaagent1)** | Blocker documented for >24 hours without resolution. | 12 hours |
| **Level 3** | **Founder Agent (webwaka007)** | Blocker unresolved by Chief of Staff for >24 hours. | 12 hours |
| **Level 4** | **Human Founder** | Critical blocker unresolved by Founder Agent for >48 hours. | 24 hours |

## 4. Escalation Process

### Step 1: Self-Resolution (Level 1)

1.  **Identify and Document:** The agent identifies a potential blocker and documents it in their personal log.
2.  **Attempt Resolution:** The agent takes proactive steps to resolve the issue. This may include:
    *   Consulting relevant documentation.
    *   Collaborating with other agents at the same level.
    *   Experimenting with alternative approaches.
3.  **Update Checklist:** If the issue persists for more than a few hours, the agent must formally document it as a blocker in their `[AGENT_ID]_CHECKLIST.md` file, including the steps already taken.

### Step 2: Escalate to Chief of Staff (Level 2)

1.  **Trigger:** If a blocker remains unresolved for more than 24 hours after being documented in the checklist, the agent must escalate it.
2.  **Action:**
    *   Update the **Master Control Board** with the blocker's status.
    *   Notify the **Chief of Staff (webwakaagent1)** via a direct message, including a link to the updated checklist.

### Step 3: Escalate to Founder Agent (Level 3)

1.  **Trigger:** If the Chief of Staff is unable to resolve the blocker within 24 hours, they are responsible for escalating it to the next level.
2.  **Action:**
    *   The Chief of Staff updates the blocker's status on the Master Control Board to reflect the escalation.
    *   The Chief of Staff notifies the **Founder Agent (webwaka007)**, providing a summary of the issue, the impact, and the resolution attempts so far.

### Step 4: Escalate to Human Founder (Level 4)

1.  **Trigger:** This is the final escalation level and should be used only for critical, system-wide blockers that the Founder Agent cannot resolve.
2.  **Action:**
    *   The Founder Agent, in consultation with the Chief of Staff, will prepare a concise brief for the **Human Founder**.
    *   The brief will be submitted through the designated communication channel, outlining the blocker and the required decision.

## 5. Responsibilities

*   **All Agents:** Responsible for attempting self-resolution, documenting blockers, and initiating Level 2 escalations.
*   **Chief of Staff (webwakaagent1):** Responsible for resolving Level 2 escalations, coordinating cross-agent support, and escalating to Level 3 when necessary.
*   **Founder Agent (webwaka007):** Responsible for resolving Level 3 escalations, providing strategic direction, and escalating to Level 4 when necessary.
*   **Human Founder:** The ultimate authority for resolving critical, unresolvable blockers.
