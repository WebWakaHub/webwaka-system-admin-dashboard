## Context & Intent (Why This Document Exists)

**Why this document exists:**
This document defines the complete set of mandatory documents that each Department Agent must produce, own, and maintain. It ensures that no department operates without clear, documented authority, scope, and accountability.

**What institutional risk it prevents:**
- Undocumented execution (agents acting without written authority)
- Scope creep and role overlap (departments claiming undefined territory)
- Knowledge loss (critical decisions existing only in conversation or memory)
- Inconsistent enforcement (different standards applied across departments)

**What breaks if it is ignored or bypassed:**
- Agents cannot bootstrap context from GitHub alone
- Governance CI cannot enforce compliance
- Future agents inherit chaos, not structure
- The platform becomes ungovernable at scale

### Department vs Role Semantics (Clarification)

**Departments are execution containers, not individual roles.**

**Key Distinctions:**

1. **Departments Are Execution Containers**
   - A Department Agent (e.g., webwakaagent1) represents an entire department
   - The department is responsible for all documents and execution within its scope
   - Departments have clear boundaries and non-overlapping authority

2. **Canonical Roles Roll Up Under Department Agents**
   - Each of the 43 Canonical Roles belongs to a specific department
   - A Department Agent may temporarily execute multiple canonical roles
   - Role execution is declared explicitly ("I am now operating as [Role Name]")

3. **Role Authority Conflicts Resolve In Favor Of:**
   1. **Founder Decisions** (highest authority, immutable)
   2. **Strategic & Governance Department** (institutional law and coherence)
   3. **Chief of Staff Escalation** (operational conflict resolution)

4. **No Role May Override Department Boundaries**
   - Roles operate within department scope only
   - Cross-department coordination requires explicit collaboration
   - Chief of Staff orchestrates cross-department work

**Example:**
- webwakaagent1 (Strategic & Governance Department) may execute:
  - Chief of Staff (Role 1)
  - Governance & Compliance Steward (Role 2)
  - Founder Decision Management Agent (Role 3)
  - Risk & Escalation Agent (Role 4)
  - Long-Term Vision Steward (Role 5)

**All roles within a department share the department's authority scope.**

## Change Classification & Approval Requirements

## Approval vs Activation

**Approval locks content.**
**Activation is a separate, explicit step declared by Strategic & Governance.**

**Approval:**
- Content is finalized and immutable
- Document is ratified by Founder
- No further modifications permitted without supersession

**Activation:**
- Document becomes enforceable
- CI enforcement rules become active
- Agents must comply with document requirements
- Declared explicitly by Chief of Staff with Founder authorization

**Timing:**
- Approval may occur before dependencies are ready
- Activation occurs only when enforcement is operationally feasible
- Gap between approval and activation is normal and expected

**Status Indicators:**
- **APPROVED:** Content locked, not yet enforceable
- **ACTIVATED:** Content locked AND enforceable
- **SUPERSEDED:** Replaced by newer version

This prevents premature enforcement while allowing governance to be locked early.
