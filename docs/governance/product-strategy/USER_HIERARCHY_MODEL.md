## 1. Introduction

This document defines the user hierarchy model for the WebWaka platform. This model establishes the different user roles and their relationships, forming the basis for the platform's access control and permission system. The hierarchy is designed to be flexible and scalable, accommodating a wide range of use cases and business models.

## 2. User Hierarchy Levels

The WebWaka user hierarchy consists of five distinct levels, each with its own set of rights and responsibilities:

### Level 1: Super Admin

*   **Description:** The Super Admin has the highest level of authority on the platform. This role is responsible for managing the overall platform, including system configuration, security, and tenant management.
*   **Key Responsibilities:**
    *   Platform-wide settings and configuration
    *   Tenant and Partner onboarding and management
    *   System monitoring and maintenance
    *   Enforcement of platform governance

### Level 2: Partners

*   **Description:** Partners are organizations that collaborate with WebWaka to resell the platform, provide value-added services, or develop new suites and modules. Partners have their own dedicated administrative interface to manage their tenants and services.
*   **Key Responsibilities:**
    *   Onboarding and managing their own tenants
    *   Providing support and training to their tenants
    *   Developing and marketing their own services on the platform

### Level 3: Tenants

*   **Description:** Tenants are the primary customers of the WebWaka platform. A tenant is an organization that uses the platform to build and manage its own digital business, such as an e-commerce store or a ride-hailing service. Each tenant has its own isolated environment with its own data, users, and configurations.
*   **Key Responsibilities:**
    *   Configuring and customizing their own instance of the platform
    *   Managing their own users, vendors, and customers
    *   Operating their business on the platform

### Level 4: Vendors

*   **Description:** Vendors are third-party sellers who operate within a tenant's marketplace. For example, in a multi-vendor e-commerce marketplace, each individual seller would be a vendor. Vendors have their own portal to manage their products, orders, and payments.
*   **Key Responsibilities:**
    *   Managing their own product listings and inventory
    *   Processing orders and managing customer communications
    *   Tracking their sales and earnings

### Level 5: End Users

*   **Description:** End Users are the customers of the tenant's business. For example, in an e-commerce store, the end users are the shoppers who buy products. In a ride-hailing service, the end users are the passengers who book rides.
*   **Key Responsibilities:**
    *   Creating and managing their own accounts
    *   Interacting with the tenant's business (e.g., buying products, booking rides)
    *   Managing their own data and preferences
